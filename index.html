Posting SOP

Standard Operating Procedure for daily social publishing

Operations · follow these steps every week

Purpose & scope

This SOP defines how PawPrint Tees plans, produces, publishes, and reviews social media content on Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook. It exists so anyone on the team can run the channel consistently and on-brand. Target output: two posts per day, balanced across platforms and the five content pillars.

Roles

RoleOwns
Content LeadWeekly plan, pillar balance, final approval
CreatorPhoto/video capture, design, editing
Community ManagerComments, DMs, UGC collection & reposting

Weekly cadence at a glance

DayActivity
MondayPlan the week; review last week's top posts
Mon–TueBatch-create photos, videos, and carousels
WednesdayWrite captions; schedule the full week
DailyEngage: reply to comments/DMs, collect UGC
FridayLog metrics; capture learnings for next week

Step-by-step procedure

1. Plan (Monday)
  • Open the content calendar and review the previous week's performance (saves, shares, clicks).
  • Assign each day two posts: set platform, content pillar, and format. Keep pillars varied across the week.
  • Confirm the posting time for each slot using the recommended windows (below).
2. Create (Mon–Tue)
  • Batch-shoot and edit assets for the whole week in one or two sessions.
  • Design carousels and graphics using brand colors (coral CTAs, navy text, cream backgrounds).
  • Keep each asset in its native format: vertical 9:16 for Reels/TikTok, 4:5 or square for carousels.
3. Write & schedule (Wednesday)
  • Draft every caption on the Hook → Value → CTA structure; put the hook in the first line.
  • Add the community tag #PawPrintPack and 3–5 relevant hashtags.
  • Proofread, then load all posts into the scheduler at their assigned times.
4. Publish & engage (daily)
  • Confirm each scheduled post went live and looks correct.
  • Reply to comments and DMs within the day; lead with warmth.
  • Screenshot or save standout customer content for future UGC posts (with permission).
5. Review (Friday)
  • Log reach, saves, shares, comments, profile visits, and link clicks for each post.
  • Note the week's top two and bottom two posts and why.
  • Carry the learnings into Monday's plan — do more of what works.

Custom pet portrait shirts for the dog-obsessed

Prepared June 2026 · Confidential

Contents

Executive Summary

PawPrint Tees turns a customer's favorite photo of their dog into a hand-illustrated, super-soft custom tee. The product is emotional, highly visual, and made for sharing — which makes social media the brand's single most important growth channel.

This report sets out a practical, repeatable social media program: who we are talking to, what we post and why, how the content is structured to stop the scroll and drive orders, the tools and workflow that keep it running, and the concrete next steps to launch. The strategy is built around five content pillars and a consistent Hook → Value → Call-to-Action framework across Instagram and TikTok, the two platforms where our millennial and Gen Z dog-parent audience is most active and most likely to share.

The goal is a feed that feels less like advertising and more like a community of people who are, frankly, obsessed with their dogs — with PawPrint Tees as the warm, fun voice at the center of it.

Brand Overview

PawPrint Tees is a custom apparel brand built on a simple, joyful idea: your dog deserves to be celebrated, and you deserve to wear them everywhere. Every shirt starts with a customer photo and is hand-illustrated by a real artist before being printed on premium, pre-shrunk cotton.

Who we serve

Our audience is millennial and Gen Z dog owners who treat their pets as family. They are digitally native, value authenticity over polish, and are quick to share content that captures how they feel about their dogs. They respond to community, humor, and genuine emotion far more than to hard selling.

Brand voice

Fun, warm, dog-obsessed, and community-driven. We sound like the customer's most enthusiastic dog-loving friend — playful and a little unhinged about how cute dogs are, but always kind and welcoming. We celebrate every dog, never talk down, and lead with delight.

All content maps to one of five pillars, ensuring variety and a healthy balance of selling, storytelling, and community:

Content Strategy

The strategy is designed to be simple enough to run consistently and structured enough to perform. It rests on four ideas: post on the right platforms, balance the five pillars, structure every post to convert, and publish at a steady, predictable cadence.

Platform roles

Instagram and TikTok are our primary channels; each plays a distinct role and the content is tailored to fit, not copy-pasted between them.

The Hook → Value → CTA framework

Every post, regardless of platform or pillar, is built on the same three-part structure. This keeps the content disciplined and makes performance easy to diagnose.

  • Hook — the first line or first two seconds must stop the scroll — a bold statement, a relatable POV, or an irresistible question.
  • Value — the middle delivers the payoff: entertainment, emotion, a useful tip, or a satisfying reveal that earns the viewer's time.
  • Call to Action — a clear, low-friction next step — usually “link in bio” to order, or a prompt to tag, comment, or share.
Pillar balance

Weekly workflow
  • Plan (Mon) — review last week’s top posts and choose the week’s themes and pillar mix.
  • Create (Mon–Tue) — batch-produce the week’s photos, videos, and carousels in one or two sittings.
  • Schedule (Wed) — write captions, finalize hooks and CTAs, and load everything into the scheduler.

  • Measure (Fri) — log key metrics weekly and feed the learnings back into next week’s plan.
What good looks like

Early on, prioritize leading indicators that signal resonance and reach: saves and shares (content worth keeping or sending to a friend), comments and tags (community engagement), and profile visits plus link clicks (purchase intent). As volume grows, connect link clicks to orders to measure return directly.
